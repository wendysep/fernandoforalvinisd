About Me

Rooted in Alvin ISD

I am a proud Alvin ISD graduate, and this district helped shape who I am today. I grew up in a hardworking, Spanish-speaking household after my parents immigrated from Mexico in pursuit of opportunity. I attended Alvin ISD schools, participated in bilingual education, and experienced firsthand the impact educators can have when they believe in their students.

I became the first in my family to graduate from college, an achievement that strengthened my belief in the power of education to create lasting opportunity.

Engineering Perspective. Proven Leadership.

I earned a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering from The University of Texas at Tyler and later a Master’s degree in Education Administration. My engineering background taught me to approach complex challenges with analytical thinking, disciplined planning, and a focus on measurable results.

I began my career in engineering, including offshore work with Chevron, before serving in federal legislative and policy roles in Washington, D.C., where I worked on research, oversight, and workforce development initiatives.

Leading in Education

I returned home to serve as an AP Physics teacher in Alvin ISD, where I was honored to be named District Rookie Teacher of the Year. I was later promoted to Science Department Head and Instructional Coach, leading instructional strategy, supporting teachers and staff, and strengthening STEM pathways for students.

Beyond the classroom, I have led large-scale STEM and workforce development initiatives at the nonprofit level, managing million-dollar budgets and partnering with school districts, universities, and industry leaders.

Family and Service

Today, I am a husband and father of three raising my children in the same district that invested in me. My wife is also an engineer, and together we believe deeply in the importance of strong schools and meaningful opportunity.

I have proudly served for four years as an Alvin Volunteer Firefighter, strengthening my commitment to safety, accountability, and stepping forward when our community needs leadership most.

About Me

I am a proud Alvin ISD graduate, and this district helped shape who I am today. My parents immigrated from Mexico in pursuit of a better life, and I grew up in a hardworking, Spanish-speaking household. I attended Alvin ISD schools, participated in bilingual education, and experienced firsthand the power of teachers who believe in their students.

I became the first in my family to graduate from college, earning a degree in Mechanical Engineering from The University of Texas at Tyler. That achievement opened doors to engineering roles, including offshore work with Chevron, and later opportunities in federal policy and legislative service in Washington, D.C. I eventually felt called back to education, returning to serve as a teacher and department leader in Alvin ISD before leading STEM and workforce development initiatives at the nonprofit level.

Today, as a husband and father of three raising my family in the same district that invested in me, I remain deeply committed to ensuring every Alvin ISD student has access to strong academics, meaningful opportunities, and a future filled with possibility.
